Hanuman Hymn: Overview

This hymn is a powerful invocation of Lord Hanuman, celebrating his unwavering devotion to Lord Rama, his unmatched physical feats, and his role as the fearless warrior who humbled Ravana and crossed the ocean to serve dharma.

Each phrase glorifies a divine quality — from leaping over yojanas to burning Lanka, from praising Lord Rama to flying with victory. May chanting this hymn bring courage, devotion, and strength to all devotees.

Phrase-by-Phrase Meaning

🕉 "Raghu Nandana Raghu Raghu Nandana Raghuvara Sevana Raghupati Chayana"
Raghu Nandana – “Son of the Raghu clan” → Refers to Lord Rama
Raghuvara Sevana – “Servant of the best of the Raghu dynasty” → Refers to Hanuman’s devotion to Rama
Raghupati Chayana – “Seeker (worshipper) of Raghupati (Lord Rama)”
✅ This line praises Hanuman’s deep devotion to Rama.
🕉 "Sata Yojana Sata Sata Yojana Sharadhini Yojana Sharapari Langhaname"
Sata Yojana – "Hundreds of yojanas" (1 yojana ≈ 8 miles)
Sharadhini Yojana – Again refers to great distances
Sharapari Langhaname – “One who crossed over distances like a flying arrow”
✅ Refers to Hanuman leaping across the ocean to Lanka, showing his power and devotion.
🕉 "Ari Bhajana Ari Ari Bhajana Arimadha Bhajana Dasha Muka Kampana"
Ari Bhajana – Devotion or praise to the enemy’s enemy (Rama is Ravana's enemy)
Arimadha Bhajana – “Worship of the destroyer of enemies”
Dasha Muka Kampana – “One who made the ten-headed (Ravana) tremble”
✅ Hanuman is glorified as the terror of evil (Ravana), and devoted to righteousness.
🕉 "Bada Bakrita Bada Bada Bakrita Bada Banala Krita Bahu Bhasmarchana"
Bada Bakrita – Possibly "mighty deeds or roars" (contextually about valor)
Bada Banala Krita – Creator of the great forest fire (possibly referring to Hanuman burning Lanka)
Bahu Bhasmarchana – “He who turned many into ashes” (again, burning Lanka)
✅ Refers to Hanuman’s destruction of Lanka after Sita’s abduction.
🕉 "Jaya Ketana Jaya Jaya Ketana Jaya Haya Prapuna Jaya Mida Dhapugane"
Jaya Ketana – “Bearer of the victory flag”
Jaya Haya Prapuna – “Victor over enemies and demons (Haya = horse, a symbol of speed/war)”
Jaya Mida Dhapugane – Possibly phonetic Sanskritization, invoking Hanuman as victorious over evil
✅ This part is a chorus of victory and praise, proclaiming Hanuman’s divine success in battle and devotion.

✨ Overall Meaning:

  • Unwavering devotion to Lord Rama (Raghu Nandana)
  • Incredible physical feats (crossing yojanas, burning Lanka)
  • Fearlessness and strength (terrifying Ravana, destroying enemies)
  • Victorious spirit (Jaya, Jaya!)
